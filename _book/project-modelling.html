3 PROJECT: Modelling

In This section we will try to understand the impact of the market value on the final ranking.

In the First part we will build the model and have a look to the parameters. In the second part we will assess the goodness of fit and see if we can predict what could happen next year

3.1 1st Section

Our model based on the following formula * points ~ log(mw) + name + year. Furthermore we will use the linear least square method to fit all the parameters.

Our model will then predict the number of points and not directly the ranking. we have thought that it is more precise and robust than direclty predict the ranking. For instance points goes from 1 to 100 and ranking from 1 to 20. However we have used the predict number of points to build a ranking and compare it with the official one.

3.2 Goodness of fit

To Discuss the goodness of the fit we will plot the residuals and have a look at some prediction made by our model.

Figure 3.1: Residuals vs Log Market value

Figure 3.2: Residuals vs fitted values

The residuals graphs 3.1 and 3.2here ar well randomly spread with a mean of zero for the log market value and for the fitted graph. It’s a good point for our model since their is no clear patern in the residuals that could provide from other information we could have miss.

In the ??,graph of our project we have build a function that take into the fitted number of points reach by a team and built a ranking with. Therefor we have built this graph to have a view on our prediction against the observed ranking. We have selected some year and championship to illustrate this, and we can see that we predict quite well the ranking with obviously some mistakes but they are quite samll in terms of ranking. In fact we have seen in the residuals graphs above that our errors are in majority under 10, and it can seems quite high when you we need approximatively 80 points to be 1st, but in terms of ranking 10 points represent around 2-4 rank. As you can see in the graph the errors in terms of ranking are quite small.
